# Target Personas

## Primary: The Founding PM
**Context:** First or second PM at a Series A–B company. Has engineering and design partners but no formal process. Spends too much time in Slack threads resolving questions that should have been answered in a document.
**Job-to-be-done:** Ship a PRD that the team will actually read and that survives the first sprint planning meeting without major revision.
**Frustrations:** Templates feel bureaucratic; blank pages feel paralyzing; reviews surface problems too late.

## Secondary: The Design Lead
**Context:** Solo designer or lead of a 2–3 person team. Owns UX research, interaction design, and design system. Regularly asked to "just make it look good" before the problem is defined.
**Job-to-be-done:** Get alignment on a UX direction before going deep on high-fidelity work.
**Frustrations:** Feedback arrives after significant investment; design decisions get undone in eng handoff.

## Tertiary: The Engineering Lead
**Context:** Staff engineer or engineering manager who doubles as the technical decision-maker. Wants to be involved early but is usually pulled in after scope is locked.
**Job-to-be-done:** Understand the technical implications of a product decision before it is committed to a roadmap.
**Frustrations:** Architectural decisions made without their input; effort estimates requested before requirements are stable.
