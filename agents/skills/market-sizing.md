# Market Sizing

## What
A structured quantitative analysis of a market's addressable opportunity, broken into TAM (Total Addressable Market), SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market), and SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market). Sizing is always accompanied by the assumptions that drive the numbers.

## How
1. Define the market boundary precisely — what problem category, geography, and buyer type are in scope
2. Choose a sizing approach:
   - Top-down: start from industry reports, apply penetration rates
   - Bottom-up: count buyers × willingness-to-pay × purchase frequency
   - Value-based: estimate value created per customer, apply pricing capture rate
3. Calculate TAM using the most appropriate approach; show the math explicitly
4. Narrow to SAM by applying realistic filters (segments you can reach with your GTM, geographies you serve)
5. Estimate SOM based on realistic win rates, competitive density, and time horizon (typically 3–5 years)
6. List all load-bearing assumptions with confidence ratings (high / medium / low)
7. Identify what primary research would most reduce uncertainty in the estimate
