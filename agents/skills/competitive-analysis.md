# Competitive Analysis

## What
A structured evaluation of the competitive landscape that maps how existing solutions address the target problem, where they fall short, and what strategic positioning is available to a new entrant or evolving product.

## How
1. Define the competitive set: direct competitors (same solution), indirect competitors (alternative approaches), and substitutes (including "do nothing")
2. For each competitor, document:
   - Primary positioning and target customer
   - Key differentiators and moats
   - Known weaknesses or unserved segments
   - Pricing model and approx. revenue tier if available
3. Build a positioning map on the two axes most meaningful to buyers (not to the product team)
4. Identify whitespace: customer needs that no current player addresses well
5. Assess competitor momentum — which players are gaining ground and why
6. Summarize strategic implications: where can this product win, where should it avoid direct competition, and what is the defensible moat over a 2-year horizon
7. Flag intelligence gaps that require primary research (customer interviews, win/loss analysis)
